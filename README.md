## Hi, I'm Kelly 👋

I'm a software developer who learns by building. I came to engineering from an
unconventional path and picked up the habit of teaching myself whatever a project
demands — most recently, low-level systems programming in Rust.

By day I work as an Apple Certified repair technician. By night I'm usually a few layers
lower in the stack than I have any business being, which is exactly where I like to be.

## About

My degree is officially in Psychology, but I designed my own interdisciplinary path through neuroscience, psychology, and computer science with a focus on applied research. I started out headed for clinical neuroscience and took a Research Assistant role at the Brain Electrophysiology Lab, where a move into test engineering rekindled a love of programming I'd carried for years.

Like many of us I learn by creating and overcoming obstacles along the way. I'd rather implement something challenging that allows me to own and understand a system than reach for something easy and stay in the dark. I ramp quickly on unfamiliar systems, and I'm driven by curiosity and the sweet satisfaction of conquering a challenge.

## What I'm building

- **[HALOS](https://github.com/kelly-gibson/halos)** — a microkernel and process-lifecycle runtime built in rust. My deep-end project for learning operating-system internals from first principles.
- SENTINEL. A supervisor, orchestrator, and state-machine machine that lives in userspace. Designed to exclusively interact with the HALOS kernel ABI surface.
- BERTH. An ELF64 parser for designed for isolation and binary-parsing safety.
- **[optimized_AVX2_quicksort](https://github.com/kelly-gibson/optimized_AVX2_quicksort)** — a quicksort built on AVX2 SIMD intrinsics, exploring how far hand-tuned partitioning, loop unrolling, and cache prefetching can be pushed.
